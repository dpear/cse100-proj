I just submitted grades for this quarter, so I've attached all of my grade spreadsheets for CSE 100 (and its remote course designation: 100R) 
for all quarters in which CSE 100R was offered. For context, here's the course description:

https://catalog.ucsd.edu/courses/CSE.html#cse100

I have removed the first few columns, which had identifiable data about the student. Thus, every row in the spreadsheet is a student, and the columns are as follows:
The red columns are the raw points for each assessment (these should be consistent in 100 and 100R across quarters)
The yellow columns are the red columns, but normalized to be a percentage as a decimal (these should be consistent in 100 and 100R across quarters)
The blue columns are the grade categories (these have changed across quarters, so they're only consistent across 100 vs. 100R in the same quarter)
The green column (Total) is the overall grade in the class, which gets translated into a letter grade separately (this, as well as the letter grade cut-offs, has changed across quarters, so it's only consistent across 100 vs. 10) in the same quarter)
Importantly, as I mentioned before, even though "CSE 100R" is the designated "Remote" version of the course and "CSE 100" is the designated "In-Person" version of the course, I told students on day 1 (and in the Syllabus) that there is literally no distinction between the two: CSE 100 students can attend remotely, and CSE 100R students can attend in-person (the course was being taught as a single large class containing the union of the two sections).

I think the most interesting analysis to start with (which would also be the simplest) would be to compare CSE 100 vs. 100R students in each individual quarter, e.g. a split violin plot where the vertical axis is total overall grade and the horizontal axis is categorical for each quarter (e.g. Fall 2022, Winter 2023, Fall 2023, etc.), and the 2 halves of the violin for a given quarter are CSE 100 (left half) and 100R (right half). 
If we notice that the CSE 100R distribution is statistically significantly lower than the CSE 100 distribution across quarters 
(e.g. using the one-sided Kolmogorov-Smirnov test), 
that would be an interesting finding for a CCSC-SW 2026 lightning talk.

In general, feel free to play around with the data to explore any questions you think are interesting, but again, 
beware of the differences in grade category and overall grade calculations across quarters 
(red and yellow columns can be reasonably compared across quarters, but blue and green can't; 
100 vs. 100R can be compared in any column in a single quarter).


- Fall 2025 (this quarter) was the only quarter in which exams were timed in-person self-scheduled closed-book proctored exams 
(Midterm had a 3 week window and was 45 minutes; 
Final had a 1 week window and was 1 hour 50 minutes). 
All other quarters had timed online open-book unproctored exams that were 
each available for a 24-hour window of time (Midterm was 1 hour; Final was 3 hours)

- Winter 2024 (last time I taught it) and Fall 2025 (this quarter) 
had the exact same grading scheme (minimum of 3 grade categories: 
Preparation = Reading Quizzes, 
Application = Programming Assignments + Projects, and 
Examination = Midterm + Final), 
so the only distinctions between these 2 quarters are 
(1) in-person proctored in Fall 2025 vs. online unproctored in Winter 2024, and 
(2) the amount of time students had to take each exam
